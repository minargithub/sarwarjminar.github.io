Biography

Sarwar J. Minar is a PhD Candidate in International Relations at Florida International University. His research focuses on global governance, international organizations, rising powers, human rights, democratization, climate change, space governance, and international relations theory.

His dissertation, The Rising Powers and Multilateral Institutions: An Investigation into the Roles of China and India at the United Nations, examines how rising powers shape multilateral institutions from within, with particular attention to norm contestation, agenda-setting, and institutional influence in the United Nations system.

Research Interests

United Nations, global governance, international organizations, rising powers, China and India, human rights, democratization, climate change, space governance, international security, peacekeeping operations, multilateral institutions, Global South politics, and international relations theory.
