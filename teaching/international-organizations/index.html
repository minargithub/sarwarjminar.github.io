INR 3502

International Organizations

Florida International University · Summer B 2026

Course Overview

This introductory course examines the origins, evolution, and effectiveness of international organizations in global politics. Students explore international cooperation, the United Nations system, regional organizations, and contemporary challenges to global governance. The course introduces major theories of international relations and applies them to understand how international organizations shape international politics.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the evolution of international organizations and global governance.
  • Apply major theories of international relations to international organizations.
  • Analyze the structure and functions of the United Nations.
  • Evaluate debates surrounding UN reform and global governance.
  • Compare the roles of major regional organizations.

Textbook and Readings

Required: Jussi M. Hanhimäki, The United Nations: A Very Short Introduction, 2nd ed., Oxford University Press.

Recommended: Tamar Gutner, International Organization in World Politics; Karns, Mingst, and Stiles, International Organization; Weiss and Wilkinson, International Organization and Global Governance; M. J. Peterson, The UN General Assembly.

Weekly Schedule

Week 1: Introduction, Key Concepts, and IR Theories
  • What are international organizations?
  • Global governance and multilateralism
  • Realist, liberal, institutionalist, and constructivist approaches
Week 2: Origins of the United Nations
  • League of Nations
  • World War II and the UN Charter
  • Principal organs and institutional design
  • United Nations Security Council
Week 3: UN General Assembly, ECOSOC, and ICJ
  • Representation and voting
  • Development and economic cooperation
  • International law and dispute settlement
Week 4: Secretariat, Secretary-General, and NGOs
  • Role of the Secretary-General
  • UN bureaucracy
  • NGOs and transnational actors
Week 5: UN Reform and Future of Global Governance
  • Security Council reform
  • Human rights and peacekeeping challenges
  • Effectiveness and legitimacy debates
Week 6: Regional Organizations
  • NATO, European Union, African Union, ASEAN, and OAS
  • Regionalism and global governance
  • Comparing institutional roles and limits

Assignments

Weekly Reading Quizzes

Short quizzes designed to encourage steady reading and concept review.

Handwritten Reflection Memos

Brief analytical memos connecting theories, readings, and class discussions.

Mini Research Project

Students collect and analyze evidence on international organizations and member-state behavior.

In-Class Activities

Simulations, debates, role-playing exercises, and group problem-solving tasks.

Grading Overview

  • Weekly Reading Quizzes: 25%
  • Weekly Handwritten Reflection Memos: 5%
  • Mini Research Project: 15%
  • Final Examination: 20%
  • Additional participation, activities, and course components follow the official Canvas syllabus.

Teaching Approach

This course emphasizes active learning through application of IR theories to IOs, debates, simulations, collaborative exercises, role-playing activities, and discussions based on real-world international organizations and United Nations documents.
