INR 3303

Foreign Policymaking

Florida International University · Spring 2025 · RVC

Course Overview

This course explores major theoretical approaches to the study of foreign policy and examines the factors, influences, and processes that shape foreign policymaking across different states. Students evaluate and analyze foreign policies through major theoretical lenses and assess the impact of different actors, institutions, and contexts in the foreign policymaking process.

Course Objectives

  • Introduce students to the spectrum of foreign policymaking.
  • Identify and discuss factors influencing foreign policy decisions.
  • Analyze and apply theoretical perspectives from International Relations.
  • Evaluate the impact of different factors in the foreign policymaking process.
  • Explain the foreign policies of different states in the contemporary world.

Textbook and Readings

Required textbook: Laura Neack, Studying Foreign Policy Comparatively. Lanham, MD: Rowman & Littlefield, 2019.

Optional reading: Cameron G. Thies, ed., The Oxford Encyclopedia of Foreign Policy Analysis. Oxford University Press, 2018.

Additional articles and book chapters are provided through weekly Canvas modules.

Weekly Topics

Module 1: Introduction to Foreign Policy Analysis
  • What is foreign policy?
  • Levels of analysis and comparative foreign policy
Module 2: Anarchy and Foreign Policy
  • Security, survival, and international constraints
  • Foreign policy under uncertainty
Module 3: Interdependence and International Organizations
  • Economic interdependence
  • International institutions and foreign policy choices
Module 4: Norms and Laws
  • International norms
  • Legal commitments and foreign policy behavior
Module 5: Leadership
  • Individual leaders
  • Beliefs, perceptions, and decision-making styles
Module 6: Small Groups and Decision-Making
  • Advisory systems
  • Groupthink and bureaucratic politics
Module 7: Domestic Politics
  • Political institutions
  • Interest groups and domestic constraints
Module 8: National Culture
  • Identity, history, and political culture
  • Strategic culture and foreign policy
Module 9: Public Opinion
  • Media, public preferences, and foreign policy accountability
Module 10: Comparative Foreign Policy Cases
  • Applying theories to real-world cases
  • Comparing state behavior
Module 11: Contemporary Foreign Policy Challenges
  • Global crises, great-power competition, and policy dilemmas

Course Components

Syllabus Quiz

A short quiz confirming course expectations, policies, and key requirements.

Weekly Discussions

Online discussions connecting theories, readings, and contemporary foreign policy cases.

Reading Quizzes

Regular quizzes designed to encourage steady engagement with weekly materials.

Final Paper

A research-based paper applying foreign policy concepts to a selected case.

Assessment Overview

  • Syllabus Quiz: 1 point
  • Weekly Discussions: 11 points
  • Weekly Reading Quizzes: 22 points
  • Abstract/Proposal of Paper: 6 points
  • Two Exams: 40 points
  • Final Paper: 20 points
  • Total: 100 points

Teaching Approach

This course emphasizes theoretical application, case-based analysis, and critical engagement with contemporary foreign policy decisions. Students connect foreign policy theories with real-world cases and develop analytical skills through discussions, quizzes, written assignments, and a final research paper.
