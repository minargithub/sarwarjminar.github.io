Biography

I am a PhD candidate in International Relations at Florida International University. My research examines rising powers, global governance, international institutions, and the roles of China and India in shaping multilateral order from within.

My dissertation focuses on how rising powers use international institutions to seek recognition, reinterpret norms, influence agendas, and reshape global governance. I use mixed methods, including qualitative document analysis, interviews, and computational text analysis of United Nations speeches and records.

Research Interests

Global governance, international organizations, rising powers, China and India, United Nations politics, human rights, space governance, climate governance, responsible power, computational text analysis.
