International Organizations (INR 3502)

Florida International University
Summer B 2026

Course Description

This introductory course examines the origins, evolution, and effectiveness of international organizations in global politics. The course explores the development of international cooperation, the United Nations system, regional organizations, and contemporary challenges to global governance. Students are introduced to major theories of international relations and apply them to understand how international organizations function in today's world.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the evolution of international organizations.
  • Explain key concepts of global governance.
  • Apply major IR theories to international organizations.
  • Analyze the structure and functions of the United Nations.
  • Evaluate debates on UN reform.
  • Compare major regional organizations.

Teaching Approach

The course emphasizes active learning through simulations, debates, collaborative exercises, policy discussions, and analysis of real-world international organizations. Students work extensively with United Nations documents and contemporary global issues.
