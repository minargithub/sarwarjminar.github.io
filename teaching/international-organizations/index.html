Course Description

This introductory course examines the origins, evolution, and effectiveness of international organizations (IOs) in global politics. Students explore the development of international cooperation, the United Nations system, regional organizations, and contemporary challenges to global governance. The course introduces major theories of international relations and applies them to understand how international organizations shape international politics.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the evolution of international organizations and global governance.
  • Understand major theories of international relations and apply them to international organizations.
  • Analyze the structure and functions of the United Nations.
  • Evaluate debates surrounding UN reform and global governance.
  • Compare the roles of major regional organizations.

Teaching Approach

This course emphasizes active learning through application of IR theories to IOs, debates, simulations, collaborative exercises, role-playing activities, and discussions based on real-world international organizations and United Nations documents. Students are encouraged to connect theoretical perspectives with contemporary global issues.
